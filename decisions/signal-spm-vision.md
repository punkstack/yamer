## 1. One-Line Product Vision

SIGNAL is the AI agency strategist that drafts, schedules, and reports — so the work lands Monday morning with or without a junior on payroll.

*(22 words — cut to 15 for pitch: "AI agency strategist that drafts, schedules, and reports every brand, every Monday.")*

---

## 2. Who This Is For

SIGNAL has three named humans in its primary orbit. Design everything for them.

### Priya — Agency PM at CEO B's shop
Priya runs content for 8 client brands out of a mid-market agency in Mumbai. She coordinates with junior strategists, manages approval cycles with clients, writes the monthly reports, and fields panicked Sunday-night messages when a post goes wrong. She uses Notion for content plans, Buffer for scheduling, ChatGPT for drafts when she's stuck, and WhatsApp for client approvals. On Monday mornings, she spends 2 hours preparing weekly content plans — one brand at a time. She is not the decision-maker who signs the SaaS contract. She is the person who makes the contract renewal a no-brainer or a fight. SIGNAL must make Priya's Monday morning dramatically better. If Priya says "I couldn't work without this," CEO B renews.

### CEO B — Agency CEO, 10–30 client brands
CEO B runs the agency. She worries about two things: client retention and junior staff turnover. A junior strategist who leaves takes institutional voice knowledge for 4–6 brands. She pays $3–6K/month per junior, and she knows she's underpriced. She has given the founder a conditional yes at $300/mo with explicit requirements: AI marketing plans, brand cycles, historical analysis, scheduling across the "famous 14 platforms," analytics. She is the buyer. SIGNAL must justify $300 against a $4K/month problem.

### Anita's Marketing Head — Client-side reviewer
"Anita" is a composite of the marketing head or brand manager at a client company — e.g., the marketing head at House of Anita Dongre, which has 2M+ Instagram followers and multiple sub-brands (Grassroot, AND, Global Desi), each with distinct voice profiles. She does not work at the agency. She is the approver. Today, the agency sends her a WhatsApp message or a Google Slides deck on Monday. She gives feedback in voice notes. She wants to see posts before they go live, request changes without hunting down the agency PM, and leave comments on specific posts. SIGNAL gives her a Reviewer seat — a read-only, approve/comment interface scoped to her brand only.

**Secondary persona (not designed for in V1, but referenced):** The solo operator who runs an in-house function for a single brand. This is Phase 3 territory — not in this Vision.

## 4. The 11 Feature Specs

### Feature 1 — Guided Intake

**What it is:** A structured onboarding flow that turns a new brand's voice, audience, taboo topics, example posts, and KPIs into a per-brand agent system prompt. Input: 6 structured questions + optional PDF upload (brand guidelines) + free-text context field. Output: a versioned system prompt that is the brain of every generation run for that brand.

**The user moment it enables:** Priya onboards a new client (e.g., AND by Anita Dongre — distinct from Grassroot, different voice) in 20 minutes. SIGNAL has enough signal to produce drafts that sound like AND, not Grassroot. The two brands share an agency but never bleed into each other's voice.

**Key UX surfaces Mia needs to design:**
- Intake wizard: 6 steps, one question per screen, progress bar, ability to save draft and return
- PDF upload field with extraction status confirmation ("22 pages read — 3,400 tokens ingested")
- Free-text context field (open, no character limit, clearly labelled "anything the questions missed")
- System prompt preview screen: agency PM can read the assembled prompt before finalising; can edit any section
- Version history: each time the system prompt is updated (e.g., new brand campaign), prior version is archived with timestamp and change summary
- Brand kit health score: a simple signal (3-star or colour indicator) showing how much brand signal the current prompt contains — flagging if Q4/Q5 (example posts) are missing

**The 6 questions (locked from PRD):**
1. Brand voice in 3–5 words
2. Target audience — one person, one paragraph
3. Taboo topics — explicit no-list
4. Three posts they love (with why)
5. Three posts they hate (with why)
6. KPIs — what "performing well" means numerically

**[OPEN] Image extraction from PDF brand kits:** Many brand guidelines are image-heavy (colour swatches, typography grids, photo direction). Text extraction captures copy but loses visual direction. Future: integrate vision model pass on PDF to extract visual brand rules. Tech feasibility TBD.

---

### Feature 2 — Multi-Channel Content Generation

**What it is:** The AI generation engine that takes a per-brand system prompt and produces a full week of posts across all channels — one call per brand per week, producing per-platform copy, hashtags, and image briefs. First-class channels in V1: Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, Threads. Extended channels: Twitter/X, YouTube Shorts description blocks, Pinterest captions.

**First-class channels (full per-platform formatting rules):**
- **Facebook:** ≤280 chars, ≤2 hashtags, no emojis unless brand kit specifies, designed for link-in-post or standalone
- **Instagram:** ≤2,200 chars main caption but punchy first line (≤100 chars) for above-fold, ≤5 hashtags in first comment (not caption), includes visual brief for image generation (Feature 3)
- **LinkedIn:** ≤3,000 chars, professional frame, ≤3 hashtags, supports "article teaser" and "thought leadership" post types distinguished by length
- **Threads:** ≤500 chars, conversational, may be a short riff on the Instagram caption or a standalone take

**Extended channels (generated but not natively published in V1 — copy produced, agency pastes):**
- Twitter/X: ≤280 chars, thread structure for long-form
- YouTube Shorts description: ≤100 chars hook + hashtags
- Pinterest: ≤500 chars, keyword-forward

**[OPEN] Channel prioritisation per brand:** Not every brand is on all channels. The intake flow should ask which channels are active. Generation and scheduling only activate for selected channels per brand. UI must handle a brand that is Facebook-only as gracefully as a brand on 7 channels.

**Per-post rationale rule (non-negotiable):** Every generated post includes a one-sentence rationale answering one of: (a) why this topic, (b) why today, (c) why this hook. If the agent cannot produce a rationale, it flags LOW CONFIDENCE and states the gap. LOW CONFIDENCE posts are visually differentiated in the review grid (Feature 5).

**Generation cadence:** Weekly by default (Monday packet). Agency PM can trigger a manual regen for a single brand at any time. Regen respects the prior week's approved posts as additional voice signal.

### Feature 3 — AI-Generated Visuals

**What it is:** For every Instagram and Facebook post in the week's calendar, SIGNAL generates a matching image using the brand's visual direction — palette, photography style, subject brief — derived from the brand kit. Output: a generated image + an art direction brief (the prompt used + a human-readable description) so the agency can override with a real photo if preferred.

**The user moment it enables:** Priya opens Monday's Instagram review and every post already has a candidate visual attached. For 60% of posts, the generated image is good enough to publish. For the other 40%, the image brief tells the agency photographer or designer exactly what to shoot. Either way, Priya does not stare at a blank Canva.

**Image style controls (per brand, set in intake and editable in brand kit settings):**
- Photography style: lifestyle / product flat-lay / artisan process / portrait / abstract
- Palette: up to 3 hex codes (extracted from PDF brand kit or entered manually)
- Subjects to include: people / hands / product only / no people
- Subjects to exclude: list of objects, settings, or demographics the brand never features
- Aspect ratio: 1:1 (IG square), 4:5 (IG portrait), 16:9 (FB/LinkedIn header)

**Regen flow:** Every generated image in the review grid has a "Regen" button and a "Replace with upload" button. Regen takes the art direction brief + a free-text refinement prompt from Priya ("make it warmer, more outdoor") and produces a new variant. Up to 3 regens per post per week before the post is flagged for human asset creation.

**Image-to-schedule gate:** An Instagram post cannot be scheduled (Feature 6) without either an AI-generated image in Accepted state or a manually uploaded image. This gate is enforced in the UI — the "Schedule" button is greyed out with a tooltip: "Add a visual to publish this post."

**Brand-kit-driven generation:** The image generation prompt is assembled from the system prompt visual fields, not free-typed. This ensures visual consistency across weeks. The agency does not write image prompts — SIGNAL does.

**[OPEN] Image generation provider:** Provider choice (DALL-E 3, Stable Diffusion, Ideogram, Flux) TBD at tech kickoff. Decision factors: per-image cost × quality for fashion/lifestyle subject matter × API availability × moderation policy for branded content. Cost per image × 7 posts/week × 4 channels × 8 brands/agency must land under the COGS ceiling of $45/agency/month. Full cost modelling deferred.

**[OPEN] Brand logo and watermark overlay:** Agencies often publish with a logo bug. Whether SIGNAL handles this in image generation or defers to the agency's post-approval workflow is TBD.

### Feature 4 — Monday Packet Email

**What it is:** The weekly compiled deliverable — strategic header + full 7-day calendar (per-day, per-channel) + performance loop signal from last week + monthly report draft (last Monday of month only) — delivered to the agency PM's inbox at 7:00 AM Monday, timezone-aware.

**The user moment it enables:** Priya wakes up, opens her phone, and the week is already drafted. She reviews in 20 minutes on the commute. She does not come into the office to start the week from scratch. The junior's Monday morning 2-hour content planning block is replaced by a 20-minute review and approval.

**Packet anatomy (locked — see Section 3 for full example):**
1. Strategic header (weekly theme / why this week / watch for)
2. 7-day calendar — per-day × per-channel, copy + rationale + visual brief
3. LOW CONFIDENCE flags inline — visually differentiated, never suppressed
4. Performance loop signal from last week (if data available via Feature 9)
5. Monthly report draft skeleton (last Monday of month only)
6. Review action link (opens Feature 5 web app)

**Email format:** Plain-text with minimal HTML — readable in Gmail, Outlook, Apple Mail, mobile clients. No image attachments in the email itself — images are in the web review platform (Feature 5). Email is the notification; the web app is the workspace.

**Sending logic:**
- Default: Monday 7:00 AM, agency timezone
- Agency PM can change send time in settings (e.g., Sunday 8 PM for a PM who wants Sunday prep time)
- Missed Monday (system error): fallback at 9:00 AM with error note prepended to packet
- If no brand has approved posts from the prior week and no performance data exists: packet generates anyway; performance section reads "No published data yet — first week in market."

**Multi-brand packaging:** If Priya manages 8 brands, she receives 8 separate emails — one per brand. Not a combined digest. Each brand deserves its own attention. The subject line includes brand name. Agency PM can opt into a "Monday digest" setting that wraps all brands into one email with section anchors (brand-by-brand) — but this is opt-in, not default.

**[OPEN] WhatsApp delivery:** Several Indian agency PMs conduct client approvals on WhatsApp. Whether SIGNAL sends a WhatsApp summary (not the full packet — just the strategic header + approve/reject link) is a growth feature. Not in V1. Log for Phase 2.

### Feature 5 — Web Review Platform

**What it is:** A browser-based content review workspace that opens from the Monday packet email link. It presents the week's posts for one brand in a grid — per-day, per-channel — with per-post actions and bulk actions. This is the primary workspace for the Agency PM and the only interface the Client Reviewer (Anita's marketing head) ever sees.

**The user moment it enables:** Priya clicks the review link from the Monday email. She sees all 14 posts for the Grassroot brand in one grid. She bulk-approves 10, sends 2 to Draft for editing, deletes 1, and edits 1 inline. Total time: 8 minutes. Client reviewer (Anita's marketing head) sees the same grid scoped to her brand, leaves 2 comments, approves the rest. Agency PM gets a notification and resolves the comments. No WhatsApp, no Google Slides, no email chain.

**Grid view — what Mia designs:**
- Columns: Days of week (Mon–Sun)
- Rows: Channels (FB / IG / LI / Threads / etc., only active channels for this brand shown)
- Each cell: post copy (truncated to first 80 chars), visual thumbnail (if generated), rationale on hover, channel icon, LOW CONFIDENCE badge if flagged
- Cell states: Pending / Accepted / Draft / Scheduled / Deleted
- Sticky header row: brand name, week range, bulk action toolbar, approval progress counter ("9 of 14 posts accepted")

**Per-post actions:**
- **Accept** — post is approved as-is; eligible for scheduling
- **Send to Draft** — post goes to an editable state; agency PM edits copy inline; visual can be replaced; re-submits for approval
- **Edit inline** — light edit mode (copy only) without going to full Draft state; for small text fixes
- **Delete** — removes post from schedule; slot is empty; system does not auto-fill
- **Regen** — triggers a new AI draft for this single post; prior version is archived

**Bulk actions (toolbar, applies to all selected posts or all posts in view):**
- Bulk Accept (select all → Accept)
- Bulk Schedule (select accepted posts → schedule per their assigned time slots)
- Bulk Delete (select → Delete with confirmation modal)
- Bulk Regen (select → regen all selected; new drafts land in same grid positions as Pending)

**Comment thread per post:** Clicking a post opens a slide-over panel with: full copy, visual, rationale, and a comment thread. Reviewer (Anita's marketing head) leaves comments here. Agency PM replies. Comments are resolved with a checkbox. A post with unresolved comments cannot be scheduled — the Schedule button is blocked with a tooltip.

**Monthly view:** Toggling from "This Week" to "Month View" shows the full calendar grid across 4–5 weeks, channel-by-channel, with colour-coded post states. Used for planning, not action. Read-only in month view.

**[OPEN] Post preview rendering:** Showing how a post will actually look on Facebook or Instagram (with profile picture, platform chrome, etc.) vs. plain text copy. Requires mock platform UI components per channel. Scope TBD — valuable for client reviewers who are not social-media-native.

### Feature 6 — Native Scheduling and Publishing

**What it is:** SIGNAL publishes approved posts directly to Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, and Threads via their respective platform APIs. No Buffer. No Later. No Hootsuite. When Priya clicks "Bulk Schedule," posts go to a publish queue with specific date/time slots. SIGNAL fires the API calls, handles errors, and retries failed posts with a notification.

**The user moment it enables:** Priya approves 14 posts on Monday morning. She clicks "Schedule Week." 14 posts are in the queue. For the rest of the week, they publish automatically at the assigned times. Priya's only job is to check the comments inbox (Feature 10) and handle any publish failures (rare, surfaced in the dashboard). She does not open Buffer. She does not open the native Facebook Business Suite. SIGNAL is the only tab open.

**This is the disruption stance.** The agency's dependency on Buffer/Later/Sprout is severed. SIGNAL is the publisher. Competitive switching cost is created the moment the first real post publishes.

**Platform API status (sourced from [Phyllo, 2026](https://www.getphyllo.com/post/instagram-api-integration-101-for-developers-of-the-creator-economy), [SociaVault, 2026](https://sociavault.com/blog/linkedin-api-free-2026), [Blotato, 2026](https://www.blotato.com/blog/social-media-api)):**

| Platform | API status | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Facebook Pages | Live — Meta Graph API | Pages-only. Personal profiles removed 2018. App Review required. BUC rate limits. |
| Instagram | Live — Instagram Graph API | Business/Creator accounts only. Basic Display API sunset Dec 2024. App Review required. |
| LinkedIn | Restricted — LinkedIn Partner Program | Opaque approval process; can take months. All third-party API access requires membership. Highest-risk platform for launch timeline. |
| Threads | Live — Threads API (Meta) | Launched 2024. Stable for publishing. |

**Scheduling time slot defaults (per-channel, editable per brand):**
- Facebook: Mon/Wed/Fri 9:30 AM IST (default)
- Instagram: Mon/Wed/Fri 11:00 AM IST (default)
- LinkedIn: Mon/Wed 8:00 AM IST (default)
- Threads: same as Instagram ±5 minutes

Agency PM can drag-and-drop posts within the calendar grid to change time slots. Changes are saved instantly and sync to the publish queue.

**Publish failure handling:**
- SIGNAL retries failed posts twice (30-min intervals)
- After 2 retries: agency PM notified in-app (red badge on dashboard) and via email
- Failed post enters "Publish Failed" state in the review grid — re-triggerable with one click
- Failure reasons surfaced: token expired / rate limit / platform API error / content policy rejection (if platform returns one)

**Token management:**
- OAuth token refresh is handled silently by SIGNAL; agency PM is never asked to re-authenticate unless the platform forces it (scope change, security event)
- Token expiry warning: 7-day notice in dashboard before token expires, with one-click re-auth
- Per-brand, per-channel token storage — one brand's expired token does not affect another brand's publishing

**[OPEN] LinkedIn Partner Program application timeline:** LinkedIn API access requires Partner Program membership, with a review process described as opaque and months-long. This is the highest-risk platform for launch. Mitigation options: (a) launch without LinkedIn native publishing, with a "copy to clipboard + open LinkedIn" manual workaround; (b) use a LinkedIn-approved third-party publisher API intermediary (Unipile, etc.) while Partner Program is pending. Decision at tech kickoff with Raj.

**[OPEN] Instagram App Review timeline:** Meta App Review for publishing permissions (pages_manage_posts, instagram_content_publish) can take 2–4 weeks. SIGNAL's developer app must be submitted before Raj starts the OAuth integration. DRI: Founder.

### Feature 7 — Multi-Brand Dashboard

**What it is:** The agency PM's home view. A single screen showing all brands under the agency's account — one row per brand — with status for the current week, last published post, next scheduled post, approval progress, and alert flags. For CEO B's agency with 8 brands, this is 8 rows. Designed to handle 50.

**The user moment it enables:** Priya opens SIGNAL on Monday morning. Before diving into any individual brand's review, she sees the dashboard. Two brands have full Monday packets ready for review. Three have approvals in progress from the client reviewer. One brand has a publish failure from last Friday. One brand has an unresolved comment blocking scheduling. One brand's system prompt has not been updated in 90 days (flagged as stale). She knows where to go first in 10 seconds.

**Dashboard columns (per brand row):**
- Brand name + logo thumbnail
- Week status: Packet Ready / In Review / Approved / Scheduled / Published / Attention Required
- Approval progress: e.g., "9/14 posts accepted"
- Next publish: channel icon + time of next scheduled post
- Alert badges: publish failure / unresolved comment / stale brand kit / LOW CONFIDENCE posts not yet reviewed
- Quick-action button: "Open Week" → opens Feature 5 review grid for this brand

**Sorting and filtering:**
- Sort by: attention required first (default) / brand name / last updated
- Filter by: week status / alert type / channel
- Search: brand name

**Scale requirement:** Dashboard must handle 50 brands per agency with no performance degradation. At 50 brands, the default view shows the first 20 rows with infinite scroll or pagination. No lazy-load jank. Mia: design for the 50-brand case, not the 8-brand demo.

**[OPEN] Multi-agency support:** An admin user (Feature 8) at a network agency that manages sub-agencies. Should the dashboard have a "agency group" level above brand? This is relevant for holding-company-style clients. Not designed in V1 but the data model must not foreclose it.

### Feature 8 — Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)

**What it is:** Three roles — Admin, Editor, Reviewer — with distinct permissions across all SIGNAL features. Admin is the agency. Editor is the agency PM (Priya). Reviewer is the client-side approver (Anita's marketing head). Roles are assigned per brand — Priya might be Editor on all 8 brands, while Anita's marketing head is Reviewer on Grassroot only.

**The user moment it enables:** CEO B sets up the agency account, invites Priya as Editor on all brands, and invites the marketing head at Anita Dongre as Reviewer on Grassroot only. Anita's marketing head logs in (or opens a magic-link from email), sees only the Grassroot review grid, leaves comments on two posts, approves the rest. She never sees AND or Global Desi. She never accidentally approves the wrong brand. She does not need to install anything or set up a SaaS account — magic link is sufficient.

**Role definitions:**

**Admin:**
- Full account control
- Create/edit/delete brands
- Invite and remove users; assign roles per brand
- Access billing
- View all brands
- Configure agency settings (send time, default channels, notification preferences)
- Approve/override any post in any state

**Editor (Agency PM — Priya):**
- Create and run intake for assigned brands
- View Monday packet and full review grid for assigned brands
- Per-post actions: Accept / Send to Draft / Edit / Delete / Regen
- Bulk actions: Bulk Accept / Bulk Schedule / Bulk Delete
- Trigger manual regen for a brand
- View performance data for assigned brands
- Draft and edit monthly reports
- Cannot add or remove users (Admin only)
- Cannot access billing (Admin only)

**Reviewer (Client-side — Anita's marketing head):**
- Read-only access to review grid for their assigned brand(s) only
- Accept posts (their acceptance is advisory — counts as "client approved"; does not schedule unless Admin/Editor confirms)
- Leave comments on individual posts
- Cannot edit post copy
- Cannot delete posts
- Cannot view other brands
- Cannot view performance data beyond their own brand
- Cannot access billing, settings, or intake
- Access via magic link (no password required) or persistent login

**[OPEN] Reviewer acceptance vs. agency acceptance:** Two-stage approval (client accepts, then agency PM schedules) vs. client acceptance directly triggers schedule. For most agencies, client acceptance is advisory — the agency PM still owns the publish decision. UI must make this explicit. Configurable per agency.

**[OPEN] Guest Reviewer link expiry:** Magic links for Reviewer access — how long are they valid? Per-week (new link per packet) vs. persistent (same link forever). Per-week is more secure; persistent is less friction for a client who accesses weekly. Configurable.

### Feature 9 — Live Performance Loop

**What it is:** After a post publishes (Feature 6), SIGNAL polls the platform API for post-level metrics — reach, engagement rate, saves, shares, CTR, comments count — and ingests them back into the brand's signal context. This data surfaces in three places: (1) the performance section of the next Monday packet's strategic header; (2) the review platform's post detail panel (showing last week's published post metrics next to this week's draft for the same slot); (3) the monthly report (Feature 11) as performance evidence.

**The user moment it enables:** It is Monday morning, week 4 of using SIGNAL for Grassroot. Priya opens the Monday packet. The strategic header reads: "Last Monday's artisan process post was your highest-reach post in 6 weeks — 4.8% engagement, 43 saves. We've weighted artisan-process content to 3 of 5 posts this week." The AI is not just drafting — it is learning what works for this brand. The strategy is improving without Priya writing a brief.

**Metrics ingested per platform:**

| Platform | Metrics pulled |
|---|---|
| Facebook | Reach, reactions, comments, shares, link clicks, CTR |
| Instagram | Reach, likes, comments, saves, shares, profile visits, story views |
| LinkedIn | Impressions, reactions, comments, reposts, clicks, CTR, follower count change |
| Threads | Replies, reposts, likes, views |

**Polling cadence:** 24-hour post publish, then 72-hour, then 7-day. Three data points per post. Cadence is configurable per agency (some want 48-hour + 30-day; default is 24h/72h/7d).

**How performance feeds strategy generation:** When the generation engine runs for a new week, it receives: (a) the brand system prompt, (b) the last 4 weeks of published posts with their 7-day performance metrics, (c) the current week's strategic context (season, campaign). Posts with >2x average engagement for the brand are labelled HIGH SIGNAL. The generation prompt instructs the model to favour HIGH SIGNAL post patterns when crafting the new week's drafts.

**Performance trend view:** In the review platform, Priya can switch from "This Week" to "Performance" — a simple chart showing engagement rate by week, per channel, for this brand. Not a full analytics dashboard. One chart. One metric per line. Designed to answer: "is the strategy improving over time?"

**[OPEN] Platform API rate limits for metrics polling:** Polling 50 brands × 7 posts × 3 time points = 1,050 API calls per week per agency. At platform rate limits, this may require queued polling vs. simultaneous. Implementation detail for Raj at tech kickoff.

**[OPEN] Instagram metrics API permissions:** Insights API for Instagram requires additional permissions (instagram_manage_insights) beyond publishing. This is a separate App Review permission. Must be included in the initial App Review submission or SIGNAL ships publishing without performance data.

### Feature 10 — Comments and Inbox Surface

**What it is:** A unified inbox inside SIGNAL that aggregates public comments from published posts across Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, and Threads — all brands, all channels — in one view. For each comment, the agent drafts a suggested reply. The Agency PM routes it (approve, edit, assign to Priya or another team member) or deletes it. Approved replies are published directly back to the post via the platform API.

**The user moment it enables (the comment-storm scenario):** Grassroot publishes the artisan process post. It lands on 4,000 accounts' feeds. By Tuesday morning, 200 comments have come in: 180 positive ("beautiful work," "where can I buy this?"), 15 questions ("do you ship internationally?"), 5 negative (one complaint about pricing, one about packaging). Without SIGNAL, Priya spends 90 minutes in 4 separate browser tabs manually reading and responding. With SIGNAL, she opens the inbox, sees 200 comments triaged into buckets, with drafted replies for all 180 positive comments (all say some variation of "thank you" in brand voice — SIGNAL batches them as "Reply with gratitude" with a preview), the 15 questions have drafted answers (based on brand FAQ from the system prompt), and the 5 negative comments are flagged red for human review. Priya reviews and approves in 15 minutes.

**Inbox view — what Mia designs:**
- Primary inbox: all comments across all brands, sorted newest-first (default) or priority-first (negative sentiment / high engagement / unanswered questions first)
- Brand filter: show one brand or all brands
- Channel filter: show one channel or all
- Post filter: show comments for one specific post
- Comment states: New / Replied / Archived / Escalated

**Per-comment actions:**
- View full post (click opens the published post in a panel — shows post copy, visual, current metrics)
- See drafted reply (agent-generated, shown inline)
- Approve reply → publishes via API
- Edit reply → inline edit → publish
- Archive (dismiss without replying — for spam or irrelevant)
- Escalate (flag for senior team member or client) → adds comment to an "Escalated" queue with optional note

**Agent reply drafting rules:**
- Replies are drafted using the brand system prompt voice — same agent that drafts posts
- Positive comment + no question → "gratitude" template variant in brand voice (Grassroot: warm, artisan-specific; AND: slightly more playful)
- Question → drafted reply drawing on brand FAQ from system prompt; flagged if no FAQ context available (LOW CONFIDENCE)
- Negative comment → NEVER auto-drafted for approval; flagged red with category label (complaint / pricing / packaging / other); Agency PM must write the reply
- Comment mentioning a competitor → flagged yellow; reply draft includes a note: "Do not mention competitor in reply — confirm brand voice before approving"

**Bulk reply approval:** For high-volume positive sentiment (e.g., 150 comments that are all "beautiful!" variants), SIGNAL groups them and offers: "Approve all 150 gratitude replies with 3 randomly varied responses in brand voice — preview?" One click approves the batch. Each reply is slightly varied (not identical) to avoid platform spam detection.

**[OPEN] DM (Direct Message) ingestion:** Platform DMs (Instagram DMs, Facebook Messenger, LinkedIn InMail) are distinct from public comments in API terms and require different permissions. Inbox V1 handles public comments only. DMs are Feature 10.5 — log for Phase 2. Must not be in the data model in a way that conflicts when DMs are added.

**[OPEN] Real-time vs. polling for comments:** True real-time comment ingestion requires webhooks (Meta Webhooks, LinkedIn push). Polling is simpler but adds latency (15-minute comment lag is acceptable; 1-hour is not). Webhook vs. polling decision at tech kickoff with Raj.

### Feature 11 — Auto-Drafted Monthly Client Report

**What it is:** On the last Monday of every month, the Monday packet for every brand includes a pre-filled monthly report skeleton — what was posted, what performed, what was learned, and what SIGNAL recommends for next month. The agency PM reviews, adds real metrics where flagged (SIGNAL fills in narrative; actual platform numbers require human confirmation), rebrands with their letterhead, and sends to the client. Agency edits the draft in under 30 minutes. Client receives the same-quality deliverable the agency would have produced in 6 hours.

**The user moment it enables:** It is the last Monday of May. Priya has 8 brands. Without SIGNAL, she spends the rest of the week writing 8 monthly reports — a task she often delivers late, sometimes delegates to a junior who writes them poorly, and always dreads. With SIGNAL, she opens Monday's 8 packets. Each one has a report draft. She reviews 4 in the morning, 4 in the afternoon, adds actual platform numbers (from native analytics or a pulled CSV), removes the SIGNAL watermark, adds the agency logo, and sends all 8. Done by 3 PM. [DataStaq AI, 2026](https://datastaqai.com/blog/agency-client-reporting-automation) documents agencies reducing monthly report time from 6 hours to 45 minutes per client. SIGNAL targets 30 minutes.

**Report anatomy (locked from PRD Section 3 / Section 3 of this doc above — see the full Grassroot example):**
1. What we posted — narrative summary of the month's content themes
2. What performed — top-performing content patterns with metric placeholders clearly labelled for human fill-in
3. What we learned — strategic insight from the month
4. Next month recommendation — SIGNAL's proposed strategic shift with rationale

**Report generation rules:**
- Narrative is generated from: brand system prompt + approved posts from the month + performance data from Feature 9 (if available)
- Metric placeholders are explicit ("AGENCY: insert actual reach number here") — SIGNAL never fabricates numbers
- First month (no performance data yet): report still generates; narrative covers strategic intent; metrics section is placeholder-only with explanation
- Agency can edit the full report inline in SIGNAL (not just in their own doc)

**Export:** One-click export as DOCX (editable in Word) or PDF. Agency applies their own letterhead in their own Word template. SIGNAL's name does not appear in the exported document. This is the agency's deliverable.

**[OPEN] Direct integration with agency's existing report template:** Some agencies have a branded PowerPoint or Google Slides template they send clients. SIGNAL exporting to a custom template (with logo, colour, fonts) would save the rebrand step. Feature scope TBD — DOCX export is the V1 path.

## 9. What Is Intentionally NOT in Vision

These are firewalled. Every feature below is out of scope for V1 design and V1 build. Mia does not design for these. Raj does not architect for these. They appear here so the team knows what to say "no" to when it comes up.

1. **Paid ads management.** SIGNAL does not touch Facebook Ads Manager, LinkedIn Campaign Manager, or any paid media. Organic social only. Paid ads have a completely different buying motion (performance marketing vs. content strategy) and a different buyer (performance marketer vs. brand strategist). Build for brand content first; ad integration is a Phase 3 play.

2. **Video editing and reels production.** SIGNAL generates YouTube Shorts description copy. It does not edit video, generate video (Sora/Runway-style), or manage a video asset library. Video production is a separate workflow requiring separate tooling. Not this product.

3. **Influencer marketplace.** No influencer discovery, no campaign management, no influencer brief generation, no influencer performance tracking. Influencer marketing is a different buyer relationship and often a different team within the agency.

4. **TikTok native publishing.** TikTok's API for third-party publishing has a separate, restrictive partner program. TikTok is an extended channel in V1 (copy drafted, agency pastes manually) but not natively published via SIGNAL. Native TikTok publish is Phase 1.5 once the core 4 channels are stable.

5. **Pinterest native publishing.** Same rationale as TikTok. Copy generated, not natively published in V1.

6. **Direct messages (DMs / Messenger / LinkedIn InMail).** The inbox surface (Feature 10) handles public comments only. DMs require separate API permissions and a different UX pattern (conversation threading vs. comment triage). Phase 2.

7. **White-label (agency-branded version of SIGNAL).** Some agencies will want to present SIGNAL as their own proprietary tool to clients. White-labelling (custom domain, agency logo in UI, custom email from-address) is a future tier. Monthly report export is already white-label-friendly (agency applies their letterhead). The web app itself is not customised per agency in V1.

8. **Multivariate post testing (A/B testing posts).** Generating 2 variants of the same post and testing performance head-to-head requires platform API integration at a depth SIGNAL does not have in V1. Performance loop (Feature 9) provides enough signal to improve strategy over weeks without formal A/B testing infrastructure.

9. **CRM or lead tracking.** SIGNAL is not a CRM. Comments and DMs that generate business leads (e.g., "how do I order?") are surfaced in the inbox with a drafted reply. Converting them to a CRM record or a deal is out of scope.

10. **Native mobile app.** V1 is a mobile-responsive web app. The review grid and dashboard are designed for mobile browser. A native iOS or Android app is Phase 3.

11. **Multi-language post generation.** V1 generates content in English (and Hindi for brands with explicit Hindi-language channels). Multi-language generation (e.g., Tamil, Marathi, Kannada) requires language-specific QA that is out of scope for V1 design and build.

Each tagged `[OPEN]`. DRI and decision deadline to be set at kickoff with Raj and Neha.

**Image Generation**

`[OPEN-IMG-1]` — Which image generation provider? Options: DALL-E 3, Ideogram, Flux (via Replicate), Stable Diffusion XL. Decision factors: per-image cost × fashion/lifestyle subject quality × moderation policy × API stability. Must land under $45 COGS/agency/month inclusive of all AI calls. Raj models cost. DRI: Raj.

`[OPEN-IMG-2]` — Brand logo overlay / watermark in generated images. In-scope for V1 or deferred? If deferred, what is the agency workaround? DRI: Founder.

`[OPEN-IMG-3]` — PDF brand kit: text extraction is table stakes (pdfminer). Visual extraction (colour swatches, photography direction examples) requires a vision model pass. Cost and complexity TBD. Deferred to Phase 2 unless trivially cheap. DRI: Raj.

**Platform API Access**

`[OPEN-API-1]` — LinkedIn Partner Program application. This is the highest-risk platform access item. Application takes months; approval is not guaranteed. Decision: (a) launch V1 without native LinkedIn publishing, with manual workaround ("copy to clipboard + open LinkedIn"); (b) use Unipile or similar approved intermediary; (c) submit application immediately and design V1 assuming approval by launch date. Founder must decide before app review submission. DRI: Founder. Deadline: before dev kickoff.

`[OPEN-API-2]` — Instagram App Review submission timing. Meta App Review for instagram_content_publish and pages_manage_posts takes 2–4 weeks. Must be submitted before Raj starts OAuth integration or it becomes the critical path blocker. DRI: Founder. Deadline: Day 1 of dev kickoff.

`[OPEN-API-3]` — Instagram Insights API permissions (instagram_manage_insights) — included in initial App Review submission or separate? Must resolve before Feature 9 (performance loop) is scoped. DRI: Raj.

**Performance Loop**

`[OPEN-PERF-1]` — Polling cadence for metrics: 24h/72h/7d default. At 50 brands × 7 posts × 3 polls = 1,050 API calls/week/agency. At 30 agencies = 31,500 calls/week. Within Meta Graph API rate limits (per-page, not per-app)? Raj to model. DRI: Raj.

`[OPEN-PERF-2]` — Real-time metrics vs. polling. Is webhook-based metrics ingestion feasible for V1 (Meta Webhooks, LinkedIn push notifications) or does polling suffice? Real-time is better for the performance loop quality; polling is simpler. Trade-off analysis at kickoff. DRI: Raj.

**Comments Inbox**

`[OPEN-INBOX-1]` — Real-time vs. polling for comments. 15-minute comment lag via polling is acceptable; 1-hour is not (comment-storm scenario). Meta Webhooks support real-time comment delivery. LinkedIn comments via webhook TBD. Raj to evaluate. DRI: Raj.

`[OPEN-INBOX-2]` — DM ingestion scope. Feature 10 is public comments only in V1. Data model must not conflict when DMs are added in Phase 2. Raj to confirm OAuthToken scopes do not over-request DM permissions in V1 (avoid platform policy risk). DRI: Raj.

**Monthly Report**

`[OPEN-REPORT-1]` — DOCX export: custom agency template support (agency uploads their Word template; SIGNAL populates it). In scope for V1 or deferred? If deferred, V1 exports SIGNAL-formatted DOCX and agency rebrands manually. DRI: Founder.

**Cost Model**

`[OPEN-COST-1]` — Full COGS model per agency per month: LLM generation tokens (weekly generation per brand × brands per agency × cost/token) + image generation (posts/week × image cost) + platform API polling + infrastructure. Must validate against $45 COGS ceiling and 85% gross margin target before dev kickoff. DRI: Raj + Founder.

**Scheduling**

`[OPEN-SCHED-1]` — LinkedIn native publishing via Partner Program vs. intermediary vs. manual fallback. Blocked on `[OPEN-API-1]`. Resolution sequence: Founder decides API strategy → Raj architects scheduling layer. Do not build the scheduling layer until this is resolved.
